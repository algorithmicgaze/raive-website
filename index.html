RAIVE AI Summer School

September 2-7, 2024

Royal Conservatory, Antwerp

RAIVE is a laboratory where young artists from diverse disciplines such as dance, music, technology, and visual arts come together to redefine the boundaries of their crafts through the lens of interdisciplinary collaboration. Here, the application of AI in art is approached much like creative writing is to literature, focusing on demystifying digital tools and hacking the conventions of traditional art-making.

The program is structured to encourage autonomy while providing access to expert guidance. Participants are divided into groups, with each benefiting from the insights of four coaches skilled in different areas of the arts. These coaches not only mentor but also share their personal artistic journeys through lectures and performances, enriching the learning atmosphere.

Introductory workshops will guide students in how to work with AI and interactive audio. These sessions empower participants to take their art in new and unexpected ways, balancing between the craft of creation and the freedom of exploration.

The culmination of this week-long journey is a public exhibition held in De Gele Zaal of the Conservatory. This exhibition isn't about presenting polished pieces but rather the raw, intriguing results of a week's exploration of art and technology. It's an invitation to the public to witness the process and the progress of works that sit at the intersection of multiple disciplines, brought to life by the next generation of artists who are as interested in the mechanics of creation as they are in the aesthetics of their final output.

Coaches

Giusy Caruso

Giusy Caruso is a pianist and artist-researcher who blends classical and contemporary music with advanced technologies like motion capture, virtual reality, and AI. As chair of the CREATION group at the Royal Conservatory of Antwerp, she explores the intersection of art, science, and technology through immersive "phygital" performances that merge physical and digital realms. Her concerts, which often feature duets with her avatar in the Metaverse, challenge traditional formats and push musical expression into new territories. Caruso's innovative work has earned recognition such as the prestigious STARTS Prize from the European Commission.

Işıl Bıçakçı

Işıl Bıçakçı (1986) was born and raised in İstanbul. After graduating from Computer Sciences and realising coding and programming was not the profession she truly aimed for, her interests started to shift towards the performing arts. After studying acting in Theatre Academy and also performing there, she started her study of Contemporary Dance at the Mimar Sinan Fine Arts University State Conservatory in Istanbul. Even before her graduation in 2015 she had started creating her own performances that were already showing her talent for inviting subtle irony and a sense of humour as well as clarity to her work, which remains recognisable in her later works.

Umut Eldem

Umut Eldem (1993) is a composer, pianist, and researcher whose work explores the artistic potential of synaesthesia, the blending of senses. He studied composition in Turkey, Italy, and Belgium, earning his Bachelor's degree from the Mimar Sinan State Conservatory in Istanbul and his Master's from the Royal Conservatoire of Antwerp. His post-graduate research at the latter institution focused on the applicability of synaesthesia and color as an inter-sensory musical concept. Eldem's multidisciplinary approach aims to create immersive experiences that transcend traditional boundaries between art forms.

Frederik De Bleser

Dr. Frederik De Bleser (º1978) is a researcher and lecturer at Sint Lucas Antwerp. His research focuses on the connection between art and technology and the development of free software tools for artificial intelligence and data visualization. He is co-founder of the Experimental Media Research Group (EMRG). He coordinates the master in digital media at Sint Lucas, and works as a freelance data visualisation artist, creating projects for Belgian news and online democracy platforms.
